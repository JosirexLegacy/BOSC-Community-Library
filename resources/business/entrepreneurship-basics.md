\# Entrepreneurship Basics — Starting With What You Have



You don't need a business school degree or startup funding to think like an entrepreneur.

What you need is a real problem worth solving, the discipline to keep going,

and a basic understanding of how businesses actually work.



\---



\## Foundational Concepts



\*\*1. The Problem-Solution Fit\*\*

Before anything else is the problem you're solving real?

Do people actually experience it? Would they pay (money, time, or attention)

to have it solved? Don't build first. Validate first.



\*\*2. Business Models\*\*

How does money flow in and out of your venture?

Common models for students and small businesses:

\- \*\*Product-based\*\*: You make something and sell it

\- \*\*Service-based\*\*: You offer skills for a fee

\- \*\*Subscription\*\*: Customers pay recurring fees

\- \*\*Freemium\*\*: Free access with paid upgrades



\*\*3. Value Proposition\*\*

What do you offer that others don't — or can't do as well?

One clear sentence that answers: \*"We help \[who] do \[what] better than \[alternative]."\*



\*\*4. Bootstrapping vs Funding\*\*

Most successful businesses in Uganda started with little capital.

Learn to build lean, test early, and reinvest before seeking outside funding.
