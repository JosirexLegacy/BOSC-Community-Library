# BOSC Community Library 

![Contributions Welcome](https://img.shields.io/badge/contributions-welcome-brightgreen.svg)
![License: MIT](https://img.shields.io/badge/License-MIT-blue.svg)
![GitHub issues](https://img.shields.io/github/issues/JosirexLegacy/BOSC-Community-Library)

A community-maintained library of academic resources, built by students
and maintained for students particularly those studying in environments
where quality learning materials are hard to find, inconsistently taught,
or scattered across the internet with no guidance on where to begin.

---

## Project Vision

Most open-source projects build software. This one builds something different:
a structured, accessible, and honestly useful collection of learning materials
for students who don't have the luxury of expensive textbooks, stable broadband,
or well-resourced university libraries.

The long-term vision is a library that covers every major undergraduate subject
area built not by a publishing house, but by students who have been through
the material and know what actually helps.

---

## Who This Is For

- University and college students in Uganda and across East Africa
- Self-taught learners looking for a structured starting point
- Students dealing with limited internet access or inconsistent course coverage
- First-time open source contributors looking for a welcoming project to join

You don't need to be an expert to use this library or to contribute to it.
You need to know something useful and be willing to share it clearly.

---

## Accessibility Mission

This project takes accessibility seriously not as a checkbox, but as a
core design constraint. Resources are chosen and written with the following
in mind:

- Works on slow or intermittent internet connections
- Readable on any device, including low-end Android phones
- Plain markdown no special apps, accounts, or sign-ups required
- Written in plain English, avoiding unnecessary jargon
- The entire library can be downloaded as a single ZIP file for offline use

See the [Low-Bandwidth Study Guide](docs/learning-paths/low-bandwidth-guide.md)
for practical guidance on using this library without reliable internet.

## Getting Started

**To find a resource:** Open **[RESOURCE_HUB.md](RESOURCE_HUB.md)**
 it lists everything in the library with descriptions and difficulty levels.

**New to university or returning after a gap:** Start with the
**[Beginner Learning Path](docs/learning-paths/beginner-path.md)**.

**Limited internet access:** Read the
**[Low-Bandwidth Study Guide](docs/learning-paths/low-bandwidth-guide.md)** first.

No account needed. No sign-up. Download the ZIP and study offline if needed.

## Community Goals

- Keep every resource free, accurate, and beginner-accessible
- Maintain a welcoming environment for first-time contributors
- Review and update resources at least once per academic year
- Expand subject coverage based on what contributors and users actually need
- Never let the library become a link dump every entry must be curated
