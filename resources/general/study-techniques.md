\# Study Techniques That Actually Work



There's a lot of advice about studying. Most of it is wrong.

Highlighting notes and re-reading textbooks \*feel\* productive but produce

very little actual learning. Here's what the research actually supports.



\---



\## Evidence-Based Methods



\*\*1. Active Recall (The Most Effective Method)\*\*

Instead of re-reading, close the book and try to recall what you just read.

Test yourself constantly. Use flashcards. The struggle of remembering

is what builds long-term memory.



\*\*2. Spaced Repetition\*\*

Review material at increasing intervals today, in 3 days, in a week, in a month.

This fights the natural forgetting curve.

Tool: \*\*Anki\*\* (free) — https://apps.ankiweb.net/

Works offline. Available on Android.



\*\*3. The Pomodoro Technique\*\*

25 minutes of focused work. 5-minute break. Repeat 4 times. Then take a longer break.

Simple, but it prevents the mental fatigue that kills study sessions.



\*\*4. Teach What You Learn\*\*

Explain a concept out loud as if teaching someone else.

If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it yet.

This is called the Feynman Technique.



\*\*5. Past Papers\*\*

For any exam, past papers are the single best preparation resource.

They show you how questions are actually asked, not just what content is covered.
