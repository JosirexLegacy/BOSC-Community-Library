\# Studying With Limited Internet Access



This guide is written for students who deal with slow, intermittent, or

expensive internet connections. That includes most students studying in

Uganda and across much of East Africa and it's a real constraint that

most learning platforms ignore.



This is not a workaround guide. It is a genuine study strategy for your

actual situation.



\---



\## The Core Principle



Slow internet is a batching problem. The solution is to do all your downloading

in one session when you have good connectivity, then study offline for as long

as possible before you need to reconnect.



Everything in this guide builds on that principle.

\## Step 3: Study Sessions Without Internet



A full study session with no internet required:



\*\*Morning, Content (45 min)\*\*

Open a resource file from your downloaded BOSC library.

Read it once for overview. Read it again, slower, taking notes.



\*\*Practice, Active Recall (20 min)\*\*

Close the file. Open a blank document or notebook.

Write everything you remember from what you just read.

Don't look at the file while doing this. The struggle is the learning.



\*\*Review, Compare and Fill Gaps (10 min)\*\*

Open the file again. Compare with your notes.

Add what you missed. Mark what you got wrong.



\*\*Flashcards, Anki (15 min)\*\*

Add the key concepts from today's session to Anki.

The app will schedule when to show them to you again.

This is how you stop forgetting things between study sessions and exams.

\## Step 4: Efficient Use of Connected Time



When you do have internet access, use it strategically not passively.



\*\*High priority (do these first):\*\*

\- Sync your Anki deck

\- Download any new materials from this library

\- Look up the specific questions you wrote down while studying offline

\- Check for feedback on any issues or PRs you have open



\*\*Low priority (avoid during limited connectivity):\*\*

\- Video streaming, high bandwidth, can wait

\- General browsing, almost always a time sink

\- Downloading large files unless specifically needed

\## A Note on Video Learning



Video lectures are valuable, but they are bandwidth-heavy and impractical

on slow connections. If you can access YouTube at all:



\- Use the \*\*Save offline\*\* feature in the YouTube app

\- Download lectures on Wi-Fi, watch at home

\- Reduce quality to 360p adequate for talking-head lectures, much smaller files



If you cannot access video at all, this library is designed to be text-first.

Every resource here is readable without video. You are not disadvantaged by

not watching videos you are just taking a different path to the same content.



\---



\## Final Thought



Limited internet is a constraint. It is not a disqualification.

Some of the most rigorous learning happens offline, with a book or a

markdown file and a notebook, away from the noise of the internet.



Use what you have. Study with what's available. The constraint is real,

but it is workable.
