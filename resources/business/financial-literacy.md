\# Financial Literacy — The Basics Every Student Should Know



Whether you plan to start a business or work for one, financial literacy is

non-negotiable. Most people learn this the hard way. You don't have to.



\---



\## Key Areas to Understand



\*\*1. Personal Budgeting\*\*

Know what comes in, what goes out, and the difference between the two.

The 50/30/20 rule is a good starting framework:

\- 50% on needs

\- 30% on wants

\- 20% on savings/debt repayment



\*\*2. Understanding Income vs Profit\*\*

Revenue is what you receive. Profit is what remains after costs.

Many businesses with high revenue are actually losing money. Know the difference.



\*\*3. Simple Bookkeeping\*\*

Track every income and expense. Use a spreadsheet if you can't afford software.

Uganda Revenue Authority (URA) has guidance for small business tax compliance:

https://www.ura.go.ug/



\*\*4. Compound Interest\*\*

The most important financial concept most people learn too late.

Money saved early grows faster than money saved later — by a significant margin.



\*\*5. Reading Basic Financial Statements\*\*

\- \*\*Income Statement\*\*: What did we earn and spend?

\- \*\*Balance Sheet\*\*: What do we own and owe?

\- \*\*Cash Flow Statement\*\*: Is actual money coming in?
